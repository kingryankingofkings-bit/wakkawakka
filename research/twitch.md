# Twitch Feature Research

This document compiles the feature research for Twitch, categorized into the reconciled 10-category taxonomy. Each category contains exactly 5 unique, specific, and actionable features, totaling 50 features.

---

## Category 1: Content Creation & Editing

### 1. Twitch Studio Broadcasting

- **Category**: Content Creation & Editing
- **Description**: A free, dedicated desktop streaming software designed by Twitch for new creators to set up their stream layout. It provides guided optimization steps to configure encoder, microphone, and webcam settings.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Stream Broadcasting Software. Download and install Twitch Studio from `twitch.tv/broadcast/studio`, log in with Twitch credentials, run the guided optimization setup to configure encoder settings, and click "Start Stream" to go live.

### 2. Twitch Soundtrack Integration

- **Category**: Content Creation & Editing
- **Description**: A tool that provides creators with a library of rights-cleared music that can be integrated into live streams. It routes the music audio to a separate channel so it does not appear in recorded VOD archives, avoiding copyright strikes.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Audio Management. Download the Soundtrack app on desktop, set up your audio routing to output Soundtrack audio to a secondary channel, and play music during live broadcasts.

### 3. Twitch Stream Marker Placement

- **Category**: Content Creation & Editing
- **Description**: Allows streamers and editors to mark specific timestamps during a live broadcast. These markers help editors quickly locate highlights and key moments when creating VOD summaries later.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Timeline Tagging. Click the "Add Stream Marker" button in the Quick Actions widget on the Creator Dashboard, or type `/marker [description]` in the chat window during a live stream.

### 4. Twitch Clips Creation Tool

- **Category**: Content Creation & Editing
- **Description**: Enables viewers and creators to capture and edit short, vertical or horizontal video segments from a live stream or VOD. These clips are saved to the platform and can be shared externally to social media channels.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Vertical Video Clips. Click the clip icon (movie clapboard) at the bottom-right of the video player, adjust the sliders to select the desired start and end times, write a title, and click "Publish."

### 5. Twitch Guest Star

- **Category**: Content Creation & Editing
- **Description**: A built-in browser-based tool that lets creators invite guests, co-hosts, or viewers directly into their stream. It automatically handles webcam and audio feeds, integrating them directly into the broadcast layout.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Collaborative Broadcast Layouts. Open the Stream Manager in the Creator Dashboard, click "Guest Star" in the quick actions menu, set up your guest slot, generate an invite link, and send it to your guest to join.

## Category 2: Content Discovery & Search

### 6. Twitch Category Browse Directory

- **Category**: Content Discovery & Search
- **Description**: A directory of live broadcasts grouped by specific video games, creative topics, or real-life activities. Users can browse categories sorted by current view counts to discover new creators.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Directory Search. Click the "Browse" tab in the top navigation bar, filter by "Categories," and click on a game or topic card (e.g., "Just Chatting") to see all active streams in that category.

### 7. Twitch Stream Tag Filtering

- **Category**: Content Discovery & Search
- **Description**: Allows users to filter the browse directories by specific creator-defined tags such as gameplay style or language. This helps viewers find niche communities and streams that match their exact interests.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Tag Filtering. Navigate to the Browse tab, click the search bar inside the browse page, type or select specific tags (e.g., "Speedrun" or "No Backseating"), and view the filtered results.

### 8. Twitch Recommendation Carousel

- **Category**: Content Discovery & Search
- **Description**: A dynamic carousel on the home page highlighting live channels, category previews, and promotional broadcasts. The content is personalized based on the user's historical viewing habits.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Personalized Discovery Feed. Navigate to `twitch.tv` (or open the home screen on mobile) and scroll or swipe left/right on the main banner carousel to preview active streams.

### 9. Twitch Live Directory Sidebar

- **Category**: Content Discovery & Search
- **Description**: A vertical sidebar on the desktop interface that lists followed active channels and recommended streams. It allows users to quickly navigate between live channels without leaving their current screen.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Sidebar Discovery. Access the left-hand panel of the desktop interface to see online followed channels, or click the collapse/expand arrow at the top-left to hide or show the sidebar.

### 10. Twitch Drops Discovery Page

- **Category**: Content Discovery & Search
- **Description**: A dedicated discovery directory showing which active streams have enabled in-game loot drops for viewers. Users can track their progress toward earning rewards by watching these channels.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Drops Directory. Open the profile menu dropdown, click "Drops & Rewards," and select "All Campaigns" to see active and upcoming in-game drops, with direct links to participating live streams.

## Category 3: Interpersonal & Community Engagement

### 11. Twitch Live Chat Window

- **Category**: Interpersonal & Community Engagement
- **Description**: A real-time, side-car text communication pane allowing viewers to chat with the creator and other audience members. It fosters community discussion and interactive engagement during live broadcasts.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Text Community Chat. Located on the right side of the video player on desktop or below the player on mobile. Type a message in the text input box and press enter to post.

### 12. Twitch Channel Points Rewards

- **Category**: Interpersonal & Community Engagement
- **Description**: A customizable rewards program where viewers earn points for watch time and redeem them for channel-specific perks. Creators can customize the point costs and reward options to fit their brand.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Channel Loyalty Rewards. Tap the circular points icon at the bottom of the chat window to open the rewards store, select a reward (e.g., "Highlight My Message"), and click redeem.

### 13. Twitch Chat Predictions

- **Category**: Interpersonal & Community Engagement
- **Description**: An interactive tool where creators set a question and viewers use their earned Channel Points to predict the outcome. It increases chat activity and viewer retention during key gameplay moments.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Interactive Chat Betting. Initiated by creators or moderators via the `/prediction` command in chat. Viewers vote by selecting an outcome panel that appears at the top of the chat window.

### 14. Twitch Chat Polls

- **Category**: Interpersonal & Community Engagement
- **Description**: Real-time interactive questionnaires launched by streamers or moderators to collect community votes on specific topics. Results are updated live in a visual bar at the top of the chat.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Audience Polls. Created by typing `/poll` in chat or via the Quick Actions panel in the Creator Dashboard. Viewers vote by clicking their desired response button in the chat overlay.

### 15. Twitch Hype Train Event

- **Category**: Interpersonal & Community Engagement
- **Description**: A community-wide celebration triggered when a surge of support (subscriptions or bits) occurs in a channel. Viewers collaborate to fill a progress bar and unlock exclusive channel emotes.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Collective Event Rewards. Triggered automatically when threshold levels of bits or subscriptions are met. Viewers participate by clicking the "Cheer" or "Subscribe" buttons on the Hype Train overlay at the top of the chat window.

## Category 4: Direct Messaging & Communication

### 16. Twitch Whispers

- **Category**: Direct Messaging & Communication
- **Description**: A private, direct messaging tool that allows two users to send private text messages to each other on the platform. It enables one-on-one communication outside of public channel chat rooms.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Private Direct Messaging. Click the Whisper icon (chat bubble with lines) in the top-right corner of the screen, search for a username, and select them to open a private chat bubble.

### 17. Twitch Chat Mentions

- **Category**: Direct Messaging & Communication
- **Description**: A chat notification mechanism that highlights a message for a specific user and alerts them inside the active chat window. It changes the message background color and plays an optional notification sound.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: User Mention Alerts. Type `@` followed by the recipient's username in the chat input, choose the name from the autocomplete dropdown, and submit the message.

### 18. Twitch Chat Whisper Command

- **Category**: Direct Messaging & Communication
- **Description**: A chat command that enables starting or sending a private Whisper message directly from the public chat feed interface. It provides a quick shortcut for users without needing to open the dedicated whisper panel.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Chat Command Whispering. Type `/w [username] [message]` in the public chat input bar of any channel and press enter to send the message privately to that user.

### 19. Twitch Whisper Block Settings

- **Category**: Direct Messaging & Communication
- **Description**: A privacy filter that blocks users who are not friends from sending private Whisper messages. It helps reduce spam and unwanted messages from strangers.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Direct Message Filtering. Go to Settings > Security and Privacy, scroll down to "Privacy," and toggle the "Block Whispers from Strangers" setting to active.

### 20. Twitch Friend List Status

- **Category**: Direct Messaging & Communication
- **Description**: A presence indicator displaying which of your followed friends are online, offline, or busy. It allows you to see what streams your friends are watching and send them direct messages.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Friend Presence Tracker. Click the Whispers button or search for friends on the left panel, view the colored presence dot (green for online), and click on their name to message them.

## Category 5: Monetization & E-Commerce

### 21. Twitch Bits and Cheering

- **Category**: Monetization & E-Commerce
- **Description**: A virtual item purchased by viewers to show support for creators in chat, which triggers animated emotes. Cheering with bits contributes directly to the creator's revenue.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Virtual Cheering Currency. Purchase Bits by clicking the Bits icon in the chat input box. Cheer during a live stream by typing `cheer100` (or selecting a Cheer emote) in the chat window.

### 22. Twitch Channel Subscriptions

- **Category**: Monetization & E-Commerce
- **Description**: A recurring monthly payment made by viewers to support a channel. Subscribing unlocks custom emotes, loyalty badges, and ad-free viewing.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Recurring Channel Support. Click the purple "Subscribe" button below the video player of a partnered/affiliated channel, select the tier, and complete checkout.

### 23. Twitch Gifted Subscriptions

- **Category**: Monetization & E-Commerce
- **Description**: Allows viewers to purchase a channel subscription for another specific user or random community members in chat. It is a popular way for viewers to support both the creator and the community.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Peer Subscription Gifting. Click the "Subscribe" (or "Gift a Sub") button, select "Gift to a specific viewer" or "Gift to the community," choose the number of subs, and complete the purchase.

### 24. Twitch Prime Gaming Subscription

- **Category**: Monetization & E-Commerce
- **Description**: A free monthly channel subscription benefit included for users who link their Amazon Prime account to Twitch. It allows Prime members to support their favorite creator at no extra cost.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Bundled Subscription Benefit. Link Amazon Prime in Twitch settings, then click the "Subscribe" button on any partner or affiliate channel, and select "Use Prime Sub" checkbox before checking out.

### 25. Twitch Turbo Subscription

- **Category**: Monetization & E-Commerce
- **Description**: A platform-wide premium monthly subscription service that provides ad-free viewing across all channels on Twitch. It also grants subscribers an exclusive chat badge and custom emote colors.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Platform Ad-Free Pass. Visit `twitch.tv/turbo`, click "Subscribe to Twitch Turbo," and complete the payment details.

## Category 6: Analytics, Business & Creator Tools

### 26. Twitch Creator Dashboard

- **Category**: Analytics, Business & Creator Tools
- **Description**: The central administration portal that provides creators with all the tools, layouts, and data feeds necessary to run a live broadcast. Streamers can customize widgets, edit stream info, and view live chat.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Stream Control Panel. Navigate to `dashboard.twitch.tv` (or select "Creator Dashboard" from the profile dropdown) to manage layouts, widgets, and stream status.

### 27. Twitch Stream Summary Analytics

- **Category**: Analytics, Business & Creator Tools
- **Description**: A post-broadcast analytics report summarizing viewer peaks, average viewership, new followers, and chat engagement metrics. It helps creators analyze stream performance trends over time.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Post-Stream Dashboard. Access this report by navigating to Creator Dashboard > Analytics > Stream Summary, where you can select specific historical stream dates to review charts.

### 28. Twitch Revenue Analytics Dashboard

- **Category**: Analytics, Business & Creator Tools
- **Description**: An itemized earnings report that displays exact revenue generated from subscriptions, advertisements, game sales, and bits. It provides creators with transparent payout logs and historical financial summaries.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Creator Income Visualizer. Navigate to Creator Dashboard > Analytics > Earnings, and use the interactive date picker to see charts of revenue sources and payout history.

### 29. Twitch Custom Emote Management

- **Category**: Analytics, Business & Creator Tools
- **Description**: A creator portal for uploading, organizing, and assigning custom subscriber emojis for various subscription tiers. It allows creators to customize their brand presence in chat.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Emote Upload Suite. Navigate to Creator Dashboard > Viewer Rewards > Emotes, select a subscription tier, drag and drop the PNG files, and click "Submit."

### 30. Twitch Streamer Ad Manager

- **Category**: Analytics, Business & Creator Tools
- **Description**: An automation tool that helps creators schedule, set up, and automate mid-roll ad breaks during their live broadcasts. It allows streamers to optimize ad revenue without manually triggering breaks.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Automated Ad Scheduling. Navigate to Creator Dashboard > Settings > Partner/Affiliate > Ad Manager, configure the ad spacing duration (e.g., 3 minutes of ads every hour), and toggle the schedule to active.

## Category 7: Privacy, Security & Safety

### 31. Twitch AutoMod

- **Category**: Privacy, Security & Safety
- **Description**: An automated content moderation system that uses machine learning to screen chat messages and hold inappropriate content. It filters messages before they appear in public chat, waiting for moderator approval.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Algorithmic Chat Moderation. Navigate to Creator Dashboard > Settings > Moderation > AutoMod Controls, and adjust the filtering sensitivity levels (1 to 4) for categories like hostility or profanity.

### 32. Twitch Moderator View

- **Category**: Privacy, Security & Safety
- **Description**: A specialized, customizable channel interface tailored for moderators to view chat, track user histories, and perform mod actions. It organizes moderation tools into a single, high-efficiency dashboard.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Moderator Command Hub. Navigate to a channel's chat, click the sword icon at the bottom of the chat panel, or go directly to `twitch.tv/moderator/[channel_name]`.

### 33. Twitch Shield Mode

- **Category**: Privacy, Security & Safety
- **Description**: An emergency button that allows streamers and moderators to instantly activate stricter chat restrictions during spam or hate attacks. It helps quickly lock down a channel to protect the community.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: One-Tap Panic Settings. Type `/shield` in chat or click the Shield icon in the chat feed or Mod View to activate pre-configured safety measures like chat phone-verification.

### 34. Twitch Ban Evasion Detection

- **Category**: Privacy, Security & Safety
- **Description**: A machine learning system that automatically scans chatters and flags users who are suspected of bypassing channel bans. It identifies alternative accounts linked to banned users to prevent harassment.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Ban Evasion Blocker. Go to Creator Dashboard > Settings > Moderation > Suspicious User Controls, and select whether to "Restrict" or "Monitor" suspected ban evaders.

### 35. Twitch Suspicious User Controls

- **Category**: Privacy, Security & Safety
- **Description**: Allows streamers and moderators to manually tag, monitor, and restrict chatters who exhibit suspicious behavior. It helps moderation teams track potential troublemakers without banning them outright.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Selective Chat Monitoring. Click a user’s name in chat to open their viewer card, click the moderation cog icon, and select "Add to monitored list" or "Add to restricted list."

## Category 8: Notifications & Time Management

### 36. Twitch Live Push Notifications

- **Category**: Notifications & Time Management
- **Description**: Instant notifications sent to mobile devices or desktop browsers when a followed channel initiates a live broadcast. It alerts viewers so they can tune in immediately.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Channel Go-Live Alerts. Open a creator's channel page, click the follow heart icon, and toggle the bell icon to enable or disable go-live notifications.

### 37. Twitch VOD Watch History

- **Category**: Notifications & Time Management
- **Description**: Tracks user progress through video-on-demand (VOD) archives, enabling them to resume long videos from the exact point they left. It provides a convenient way to watch long broadcasts in multiple sessions.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Resume Video Progress. Navigate to the Twitch homepage or mobile application and view the "Continue Watching" row to click and load active video history.

### 38. Twitch Snooze Live Notifications

- **Category**: Notifications & Time Management
- **Description**: A utility that lets viewers temporarily snooze or silence push notifications for followed channels. It helps users manage interruptions during busy or quiet periods of their day.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Push Notification Muting. Tap and hold a Twitch push notification on your mobile lock screen, and select the option to snooze go-live notifications for the next 1, 2, or 24 hours.

### 39. Twitch Chat Feed Pausing

- **Category**: Notifications & Time Management
- **Description**: Stops the live scrolling of chat when a user scrolls up, preventing them from losing their place. It is useful for reading older messages in fast-moving chat rooms.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Manual Chat Review. Scroll up inside the chat pane during a high-activity live stream; a "Chat Paused" banner will appear at the bottom. Scroll to the bottom to resume scrolling.

### 40. Twitch Email Broadcast Alerts

- **Category**: Notifications & Time Management
- **Description**: Automated email notifications dispatched to the user's inbox summarizing active stream categories, titles, and thumbnails. It keeps offline users updated on their favorite streamers' schedules.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Email Alert Preferences. Go to Settings > Notifications > Email, and toggle the "Live Broadcast" alerts checkbox on or off for the entire account or specific channels.

## Category 9: Developer APIs & Integrations

### 41. Twitch API Helix

- **Category**: Developer APIs & Integrations
- **Description**: Programmatic HTTP interface (Helix) allowing developers to query live stream statuses, fetch user profiles, and search games. It is the primary REST API used to integrate Twitch features into third-party apps.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: RESTful API Endpoint. Log in to the Twitch Developer Portal, register an application to generate a Client ID and secret, obtain an OAuth token, and perform GET requests to endpoints like `https://api.twitch.tv/helix/streams`.

### 42. Twitch EventSub Webhooks

- **Category**: Developer APIs & Integrations
- **Description**: A subscription-based webhook service that pushes real-time JSON event payloads to developer servers for events like new followers or subs. It eliminates the need for developers to poll the API for changes.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Real-time Webhooks. Send a POST request to `https://api.twitch.tv/helix/eventsub/subscriptions` specifying the event type (e.g., `channel.follow`), callback URL, and signature secret.

### 43. Twitch Chat WebSocket Protocol

- **Category**: Developer APIs & Integrations
- **Description**: A WebSocket interface using IRC protocol standards that allows custom chat bots to read, write, and manage messages. It enables programmatic interaction with live stream chat rooms.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: WebSocket Chat Connection. Establish a connection to `wss://irc-ws.chat.twitch.tv:443` via an IRC client or script, send credentials (`PASS oauth:token` and `NICK username`), and join channels to listen for events.

### 44. Twitch Extensions SDK

- **Category**: Developer APIs & Integrations
- **Description**: A JavaScript helper library and developer framework that allows building interactive web-overlays or panels. It lets developers build interactive mini-apps directly on the video player.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Interactive Overlay SDK. Create a project in the Twitch Developer console, import the `twitch-ext.min.js` script into your HTML/JS overlay bundle, and utilize the `window.Twitch.ext` listener.

### 45. Twitch OAuth 2.0 Authentication Service

- **Category**: Developer APIs & Integrations
- **Description**: The industry-standard secure authentication flow used by developers to request specific permission scopes from Twitch users. It secures user data while allowing third-party tools to access necessary account functions.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Federated Authentication API. Direct users to `https://id.twitch.tv/oauth2/authorize` with query parameters specifying `client_id`, `redirect_uri`, and required permission `scope` to receive authorization codes.

## Category 10: Account Settings & Authentication

### 46. Twitch Two-Factor Authentication

- **Category**: Account Settings & Authentication
- **Description**: A security setting requiring users to enter a mobile verification code in addition to their password during login. It protects accounts from unauthorized access attempts and credential leaks.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Account Security Verification. Go to Settings > Security and Privacy > Two-Factor Authentication, click "Set Up Two-Factor Authentication," verify your phone number, and connect an app like Authy or Google Authenticator.

### 47. Twitch Connected Third-Party Accounts

- **Category**: Account Settings & Authentication
- **Description**: Allows users to link their Twitch account to third-party services like Amazon, Discord, Steam, and Battle.net. It enables cross-platform game rewards, sharing subscription statuses, and profile integrations.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Federated Account Linking. Go to Settings > Connections, locate the desired third-party application, click the "Connect" button, and approve the connection in the pop-up window.

### 48. Twitch Login Device History

- **Category**: Account Settings & Authentication
- **Description**: An audit log displaying recent devices, browser types, dates, and geographic locations that have logged into the account. It allows users to monitor account activity and revoke access to unrecognized devices.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Security Device Management. Navigate to Settings > Security and Privacy, scroll to "Security," and inspect the active list of devices or click "Log Out From All Devices" to revoke access.

### 49. Twitch Username Cooldown Release

- **Category**: Account Settings & Authentication
- **Description**: A settings control that allows users to change their display username, releasing their old name back into the pool after a cooldown. It helps users rebrand their accounts while maintaining their existing followers.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Account Re-naming. Go to Settings > Profile, click the edit pencil icon next to the "Username" field, enter a new available username, and click "Save."

### 50. Twitch Creator Agreement Dashboard

- **Category**: Account Settings & Authentication
- **Description**: An account portal containing the Twitch Affiliate or Partner contracts, tax documents, and payout preference selections. It allows streamers to review their legal agreements and update payment methods.
- **Sub-category / Actionable Details**: Creator Agreement Management. Navigate to Creator Dashboard > Settings > Partner or Affiliate, click on "Affiliate Onboarding," and fill out or review the active tax and payout information forms.
